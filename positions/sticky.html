CSS Positioning: Sticky

The yellow box below uses position: sticky. It behaves like position: relative until you scroll within its parent. Then, it becomes fixed at top: 10px relative to the parent.

Key Points:

  • position: sticky is scoped to the element's nearest scrollable ancestor (even if it doesn't have position: relative).
  • Sticky elements behave like relative until they reach a defined threshold (top, bottom, etc.).
  • After reaching the threshold, they become fixed until the parent boundary is crossed.
  • If the parent is smaller than the threshold, sticky behavior may not work as expected.

Tip: Use position: sticky for elements that should stick temporarily within a scrollable container, like headers or sidebars.
