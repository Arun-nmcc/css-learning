More content to scroll through. Fixed positioning is great for elements like sticky headers, navigation bars, or call-to-action buttons.

Key Points:

  • position: fixed removes the element from the document flow, similar to absolute.
  • Unlike absolute, the element is positioned relative to the viewport and does not move when the page scrolls.
  • Fixed elements are not block-level by default; they shrink-wrap their content.
  • Use z-index to ensure fixed elements stay on top of other content.

Tip: Use position: fixed for sticky headers, floating buttons, or sidebars that need to stay visible during scrolling.
